Data

According to data from 2021, the state of the water in the Philippines shows that 56% of the nation's major water resources are polluted, 43% of which are rivers, accounting to a total of 99% of polluted body waters. The availability of water in the Philippines is alarming that the devastating problem causes the water to change in quality

However, one thing to note is that water pollution does not only affect those organisms inhabiting a particular area, like the marine organisms. This also results in affecting a rural community or area that lacks access to clean water. Despite the vast population of the country with over 119,106,224 people, a growth rate of 1.51% from last year, at least 91% of households have the opportunity to access basic water services leaving 8% behind. On the other hand, there are some regions that have access as low as 62% making it still inadequate as 52% of some households in the Philippines do not have access to water supply.

The Senate of the Philippines (SEPO 2023), stated that the Annual Poverty Indicators Survey 2022 indicated that there are at least 96.3% equivalent to 25.9 million families that can access safe drinking water from improved water sources. However there are 2.4% (646,608 families) left behind with safe water sources, like surface water and unprotected wells.
